Where it slips

The six common leaks

Most businesses don't have a marketing problem — they have a systems problem. The jobs are already calling; they're leaking out between the first touch and the invoice.

The missed call

Someone called, nobody answered, and nobody texted them back. They booked the next company that did.

The quiet quote

You sent the estimate and never followed up. Most quotes don't die on price — they die in silence.

Leads in the inbox

Form fills and DMs sitting in an inbox with no owner, no next step, and no follow-up sequence.

No pipeline

Work lives in your head, a notebook, and three group chats — so nothing has a status and nothing gets chased.

Reviews never asked

Happy customers finish the job and leave — and the review that wins the next ten jobs never gets requested.

Customers never revisited

Past customers who already trust you never hear from you again. That list is the cheapest revenue you have.
